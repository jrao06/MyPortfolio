Web Application

Dollar Dash Thrills Hackers With Live Market Engine

InnovationHACKS 2.0 · Arizona State University

DOLLAR DASH is a real-time multiplayer stock trading game conceived and built for InnovationHACKS 2.0 at ASU. The system supports simultaneous players via WebSockets, with a live market engine updating prices four times per second, driven by volatility parameters, drift coefficients, and event-based news injections.

The project showcases Mr. Rao's mastery of the full stack: a React 19 front-end, a Node.js server, and Socket.io transport layer capable of handling the rapid-fire price propagation demanded by live gameplay.

SpaceHACK Entry Conquers 150 Teams, Claims Two Prizes

SpaceHACK 2026 · 1st ASU · 2nd International

COMPETING AGAINST more than 150 teams worldwide at SpaceHACK 2026, Mr. Rao's entry placed first at ASU and second internationally. The project harnessed Google Earth Engine, Sentinel-5P carbon monoxide datasets, SRTM elevation data, and live vessel-tracking feeds.

The objective — identifying the most efficient overland freight corridors to reduce emissions — demanded both sophisticated geospatial reasoning and fluency with satellite APIs rarely encountered by first-year undergraduates.

Accessibility Hardware

Braille Display Links Three Continents in Accessibility Push

EPICS Program · Arizona State University · Jan 2026 – Present

AS PROTOTYPING LEAD on the Africa2Bridge Braille Display project, Mr. Rao is working to improve web accessibility for blind and partially sighted users worldwide. The device combines AI-driven software with affordable braille display hardware.

The effort brings together Tetragon.co of Austria, Prinshof School in South Africa, and the EPICS program at ASU — a partnership spanning three continents and united by the conviction that assistive technology should not be a luxury.
